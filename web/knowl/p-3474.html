We now develop explicit formulas for the real and imaginary parts of the principal value of the arcsine function \(w=f(z) =\mathrm{Arcsin} z\text{.}\) We use this mapping to solve problems involving steady temperatures and ideal fluid flow in Section 9.7. The mapping is found by solving the equation
\begin{equation} x+iy = \sin w = \sin u\cosh v + i\cos u\sinh v\tag{9.4.3} \end{equation}
for \(u\) and \(v\) expressed as functions of \(x\) and \(y\text{.}\) To solve for \(u\text{,}\) we first equate the real and imaginary parts of Equation (9.4.3) and obtain the equations
\begin{equation*} \cosh v = \frac{x}{\sin u}, \text{ and } \sinh v=\frac{y}{\cos u} \end{equation*}
