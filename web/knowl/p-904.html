The geometric description of the reciprocal transformation is now evident. It is an inversion (that is, the modulus of \(\frac{1}{z}\) is the reciprocal of the modulus of \(z\)) followed by a reflection through the \(x\) axis. The ray \(r>0, \, \theta =\alpha\text{,}\) is mapped one-to-one and onto the ray \(\rho >0, \, \phi = -\alpha\text{.}\) Points that lie inside the unit circle \(C_1(0) =\{z:|z|=1\}\) are mapped onto points that lie outside the unit circle and vice versa, as FigureÂ 2.5.1 illustrates.
