The deformation of contour theorem implies that the integral of \(f\) over \(C_R\) has the same value as the integral of \(f\) over \(C\text{,}\) so
\begin{equation*} \int_C\frac{1}{z-z_0}\,dz = \int_{C_R}\frac{1}{z-z_0}\,dz =\int_0^{2\pi}\frac{iRe^{i\theta}}{Re^{i\theta}}\,d\theta =i\int_0^{2\pi}\,d\theta =2\pi i \end{equation*}
and
\begin{align*} \int_C\frac{1}{(z-z_0)^n}\,dz \amp = \int_{C_R}\frac{1}{(z-z_0)^n}\,dz = \int_0^{2\pi}\frac{iRe^{i\theta}}{R^ne^{in\theta}}\,d\theta =iR^{1-n}\int_0^{2\pi}e^{i(1-n) \theta}\ d\theta\\ \amp = \left.\frac{R^{1-n}}{1-n}e^{i(1-n) \theta}\right|_{\theta =0}^{\theta =2\pi}=\frac{R^{1-n}}{1-n}-\frac{R^{1-n}}{1-n}=0\text{.} \end{align*}
