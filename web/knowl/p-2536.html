Corollary 7.2.5 clears up what often seems to be a mystery when series are first introduced in calculus. The calculus analog of Equations (7.2.8) is
\begin{equation} \frac{1}{1-x^2} = \sum\limits_{n=0}^{\infty}x^{2n} \text{ and } \frac{1}{1+x^2} = \sum\limits_{n=0}^{\infty}(-1)^nx^{2n} \text{ for } x \in (-1,1)\text{.}\tag{7.2.10} \end{equation}
