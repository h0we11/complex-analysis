Proof

According to Equation (7.3.1),
\begin{equation*} \sum_{n=- \infty}^{\infty}c_n(z-\alpha)^n = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}c_{-n}(z-\alpha)^{-n} + \sum_{n=0}^{\infty}c_n(z-\alpha)^n\text{.} \end{equation*}
By TheoremÂ 7.1.5, the series \(\sum\limits_{n=0}^{\infty}c_n(z-\alpha)^n\) must converge uniformly on the closed disk \(\overline{D}_{t}(\alpha)\text{.}\) By the Weierstrass \(M\)-test, we can show that the series \(\sum\limits_{n=1}^{\infty}c_{-n}(z-\alpha)^{-n}\) converges uniformly on \(\{z:|z-\alpha| \ge s\}\) (we leave the details as an exercise). Combining these two facts yields the required result.
