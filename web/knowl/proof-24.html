Proof

The point \(z_0\) is a fixed point for \(f_c\) iff \(f_c(z_0)=z_0\text{.}\) In other words, iff \(z_0^2-z_0+c = 0\text{.}\) By TheoremÂ 1.5.12, the solutions to this equation are
\begin{equation*} z_0 = \frac{1+\sqrt{1-4c}}{2} \text{ or } z_0 = \frac{1-\sqrt{1-4c}}{2}\text{,} \end{equation*}
where again the square root designates the principal square root function. Now, \(z_0\) is an attracting point iff \(|f\,_c'(z_0)| = |2z_0| \lt 1\text{.}\) Combining this result with the solutions for \(z_0\) gives our desired result.
