(Cauchy ’s proof of Theorem 6.3.7.) If we suppose that \(f\,'\) is continuous, then with \(C\) oriented positively we use Equation (6.2.7) to write
\begin{equation} \int_Cf(z)\,dz = \int_Cu\ dx-v\,dy+i\int_Cv\,dx + u\,dy\text{.}\tag{6.3.4} \end{equation}
If we use Green’s theorem on the real part of the right side of Equation (6.3.4) (with \(P=u\) and \(Q=-v)\text{,}\) we obtain
\begin{equation} \int_Cu\,dx - v\,dy = \iint\limits_R(-v_x-u_y)\,dx\,dy\text{,}\tag{6.3.5} \end{equation}
where \(R\) is the region that is the interior of \(C\text{.}\) If we use Green’s theorem on the imaginary part, we get
\begin{equation} \int_Cv\,dx +u\,dy = \iint\limits_R(u_x-v_y)\,dx\,dy\text{.}\tag{6.3.6} \end{equation}
If we use the Cauchy-Riemann equations \(u_x=v_y\) and \(u_y=-v_x\) in Equations (6.3.5) and (6.3.6), Equation (6.3.4) becomes
\begin{equation*} \int_Cf(z)\,dz = \iint\limits_R0\,dx\ dy +i\iint\limits_R0\,dx\,dy=0\text{,} \end{equation*}
which completes the proof.
