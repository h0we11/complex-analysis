Theorem 2.3.7.

Let \(f(z) = u(x, y) +iv(x, y)\) be a complex function that is defined in some neighborhood of \(z_0\text{,}\) except perhaps at \(z_0=x_0+iy_0\text{.}\) Then
\begin{equation} \lim\limits_{z \to z_0}\,f(z) =w_0=u_0+iv_0\tag{2.3.2} \end{equation}
iff
\begin{equation} \lim\limits_{(x,y) \to (x_0,y_0)} u(x, y)=u_0 \text{ and } \lim\limits_{(x,y) \to (x_0,y_0)} v(x, y)=v_0\text{.}\tag{2.3.3} \end{equation}
