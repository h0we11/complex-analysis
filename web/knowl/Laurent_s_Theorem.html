Theorem 7.3.6. Laurent’s theorem.

Suppose that \(0 \le r\lt R\text{,}\) and that \(f\) is analytic in the annulus \(A=A(\alpha,r,R)\text{.}\) If \(\rho\) is any number such that \(r \lt \rho \lt R\text{,}\) then for all \(z_0 \in A \, f\) has the Laurent series representation
\begin{equation} f(z_0) = \sum_{n=- \infty}^{\infty}c_n(z_0-\alpha)^n = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}c_{-n}(z_0-\alpha)^{-n} + \sum_{n=0}^{\infty}c_n(z_0-\alpha)^n\text{,}\tag{7.3.2} \end{equation}
where for \(n=0,\,1,\,2,\ldots\text{,}\) the coefficients \(c_{-n}\) and \(c_n\) are given by
\begin{equation} c_{-n}=\frac{1}{2\pi i} \int_{C_{\rho }^{+}(\alpha)}\frac{f(z)}{(z-\alpha)^{-n+1}}\,dz \text{ and } c_n=\frac{1}{2\pi i} \int_{C_{\rho }^{+}(\alpha)}\frac{f(z)}{(z-\alpha)^{n+1}}\,dz\text{.}\tag{7.3.3} \end{equation}
Moreover, the convergence in Equation (7.3.2) is uniform on any closed subannulus \(\overline{A}(\alpha ,s,t)\text{,}\) where \(r\lt s\lt t\lt R\text{.}\)
