Example 6.2.8.

Use a Riemann sum to approximate the integral \(\int_C\exp\) \(z\ dt\text{,}\) where \(C\) is the line segment joining the point \(A=0\) to \(B=2+i\frac{\pi}{4}\text{.}\)
Solution.
Set \(n=8\) in Equation (6.2.3) and form the partition \(P_8:z_k= \frac{k}{4}+i\frac{\pi k}{32}\) for \(k=0,\,1,\,2,\ldots ,\,8\text{.}\) For this situation, we have a uniform increment \(\Delta z_k=\frac{1}{4}+i\frac{\pi}{32}\text{.}\) For convenience we select \(c_k=\frac{z_{k-1}+z_k}{2}=\frac{2k-1}{ 8}+i\frac{\pi(2k-1)}{64}\text{,}\) for \(k=1,\,2,\ldots ,\,8\text{.}\) FigureÂ 6.2.9 shows the points \(\{z_k\}\) and \(\{c_k\}\text{.}\)
One possible Riemann sum, then, is
\begin{equation*} S(P_8)=\sum_{k=1}^8f(c_k) \Delta z_k = \sum\limits_{k=1}^{8}\exp \left[\frac{2k-1}{8}+i\frac{\pi(2k-1)}{64}\right]\left(\frac{1}{4}+i\frac{\pi}{32}\right)\text{.} \end{equation*}
By rounding the terms in this Riemann sum to two decimal digits, we obtain an approximation for the integral:
\begin{align*} S(P_8) \amp \approx (0.28+0.13i) +(0.33+0.19i) +(0.41+0.29i) +(0.49+0.42i)\\ \amp + (0.57+0.6i) +(0.65+0.84i) +(0.72+1.16i) +(0.78+1.57i)\\ \amp \approx 4.23+5.20i\text{.} \end{align*}
This result compares favorably with the precise value of the integral, which you will soon see equals
\begin{equation*} \exp\left(2+i\frac{\pi}{4}\right) -1 = -1+e^2\left(\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\right)+ie^2\left(\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\right) \approx 4.22485+5.22485i \end{equation*}
