Theorem 7.5.5. Division of power series.

Suppose that \(f\) and \(g\) are analytic at \(\alpha\) with the power series representations
\begin{equation*} f(z) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty}a_n(z-\alpha)^n \text{ and } g(z) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty}b_n(z-\alpha)^n, \text{ for all } z \in D_{R}(\alpha)\text{.} \end{equation*}
If \(g(\alpha) \ne 0\text{,}\) then the quotient \(\frac{f}{g}\) has the power series representation
\begin{equation*} \frac{f(z)}{g(z)} = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty}c_n(z-\alpha)^n\text{,} \end{equation*}
where the coefficients satisfy the equation
\begin{equation*} a_n = b_0c_n + \cdots + b_{n-1}c_1 + b_nc_0\text{.} \end{equation*}
In other words, we can obtain the series for the quotient \(\frac{f(z)}{g(z)}\) by the familiar process of dividing the series for \(f(z)\) by the series for \(g(z)\text{,}\) using the standard long division algorithm.
