Example 11.6.6.

Solve the initial value problem
\begin{equation*} y\,''(t) + y(t) = 0 \text{ with } y(0) =2 \text{ and } y\,'(0) = 3\text{.} \end{equation*}
Solution.
Since the right-hand side of the differential equation is \(f(t) \equiv 0\) we have \(F(s) \equiv 0\text{.}\) The initial conditions yield \(\mathcal{L}\big(y\,''(t)\big) =s^2Y(s) - 2s- 3\) and Equation (11.6.1) becomes \(s^2Y(s) + Y(s) = 2s + 3\text{.}\) Solving we get \(Y(s) =\frac{2s+3}{s^2+1}\) and the solution \(y(t)\) is assisted by using TableÂ 11.5.10 and the computation
\begin{equation*} y(t) = \mathcal{L}^{-1}\left(\frac{2s+3}{s^2+1}\right) = 2\mathcal{L}^{-1}\left(\frac{s}{s^2+1}\right) + 3\mathcal{L}^{-1}\left(\frac{1}{s^2+1}\right) =2\cos t+3\sin t\text{.} \end{equation*}
