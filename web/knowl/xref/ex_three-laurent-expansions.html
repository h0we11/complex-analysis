Example 7.3.9.

Find three different Laurent series representations for \(f(z) =\frac{3}{2+z-z^2 }\) involving powers of \(z\text{.}\)
Solution.
The function \(f\) has singularities at \(z=-1,2\) and is analytic in the disk \(D:|z| \lt 1\text{,}\) in the annulus \(A:1\lt |z| \lt 2\text{,}\) and in the region \(R:|z| >2\text{.}\) We want to find a different Laurent series for \(f\) in each of the three domains \(D\text{,}\) \(A\text{,}\) and \(R\text{.}\) We start by writing \(f\) in its partial fraction form:
\begin{equation} f(z) = \frac{3}{(1+z) (2-z)} = \frac{1}{1+z} + \frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{1}{1-\frac{z}{2}}\right)\text{.}\tag{7.3.10} \end{equation}
We use Theorem 4.3.1 and Corollary 4.3.2 to obtain the following representations for the terms on the right side of Equation (7.3.10):
\begin{align} \frac{1}{1+z} \amp = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty}(-1)^nz^n, \amp \amp \text{ valid for } |z| \lt 1,\tag{7.3.11}\\ \frac{1}{1+z} \amp = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\frac{(-1)^{n+1}}{z^n}, \amp \amp \text{ valid for } |z| >1,\tag{7.3.12}\\ \frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{1}{1-\frac{z}{2}}\right) \amp = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty}\frac{z^n}{2^{n+1}}, \amp \amp \text{ valid for } |z| \lt 2, \text{ and }\tag{7.3.13}\\ \frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{1}{1-\frac{z}{2}}\right) \amp = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\frac{-2^{n-1}}{z^n}, \amp \amp \text{ valid for } |z| >2\text{.}\tag{7.3.14} \end{align}
Representations (7.3.11) and (7.3.13) are both valid in the disk \(D\text{,}\) and thus we have
\begin{equation*} f(z) =\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}\left[(-1)^n+\frac{1}{2^{n+1}}\right] z^n, \text{ valid for } |z| \lt 1\text{,} \end{equation*}
which is a Laurent series that reduces to a Maclaurin series. In the annulus \(A\text{,}\) Representations (7.3.12) and (7.3.13) are valid; hence we get
\begin{equation*} f(z) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\frac{(-1)^{n+1}}{z^n}+\sum\limits_{n=0}^{\infty}\frac{z^n}{2^{n+1}}, \text{ valid for } 1\lt |z| \lt 2\text{.} \end{equation*}
Finally, in the region \(R\) we use Representations (7.3.12) and (7.3.14) to obtain
\begin{equation*} f(z) =\sum\limits_{n=1}^{\infty}\frac{(-1)^{n+1}-2^{n-1}}{z^n}, \text{ valid for } |z| >2\text{.} \end{equation*}
