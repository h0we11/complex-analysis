Let
\begin{equation*} F_1(w) = \Phi(u,v) + i\Psi(u,v) \end{equation*}
denote the complex potential for a fluid flow in a domain \(G\) in the \(w\) plane, where the velocity is
\begin{equation*} \mathbf{V}_1(u,v) = \overline{F_1,'(w)}\text{.} \end{equation*}
If the function \(w=S(z)=u(x,y)+iv(x,y)\) is a one-to-one conformal mapping from a domain \(D\) in the \(z\)-plane onto \(G\text{,}\) then the composite function
\begin{equation*} F_2(z) = F_1(S(z)) = \Phi\big(u(x,y), \, v(x,y)\big) + i\Psi\big(u(x,y), \, v(x,y)\big) \end{equation*}
is the complex potential for a fluid flow in \(D\text{,}\) where the velocity is
\begin{equation*} \mathbf{V}_2(x,y) = \overline{F_2,'(z)}\text{.} \end{equation*}
