Because the ordered triple of points \(w_1\text{,}\) \(w_2\text{,}\) and \(w_3\) lie on the \(u\)-axis, it follows that the image of circle \(C\) is the \(u\)-axis. The points \(w_1\text{,}\) \(w_2\text{,}\) and \(w_3\) give the upper half-plane \(G\text{:}\) \(\mathrm{ Im}(w) >0\) a left orientation. Therefore \(w=S(z)\) maps the disk \(D\) onto the upper half-plane \(G\text{.}\) To check our work, we choose a point \(z_0\) that lies in \(D\) and find the half-plane in which its image, \(w_0\text{,}\) lies.
