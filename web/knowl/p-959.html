Using our imagination, we take our lead from elementary calculus and define the derivative of \(f\) at \(z_0\text{,}\) written \(f\,'(z_0)\text{,}\) by
\begin{equation} f\,'(z_0) = \lim\limits_{z \to z_0}\frac{f(z) -f(z_0)}{z-z_0}\text{,}\tag{3.1.1} \end{equation}
provided the limit exists. If it does, we say that the function \(f\) is differentiable at \(z_0\text{.}\) If we write \(\Delta z=z-z_0\text{,}\) then we can express Equation (3.1.1) in the form
\begin{equation} f\,'(z_0) = \lim\limits_{\Delta z \to 0}\frac{f(z_0+\Delta z) -f(z_0)}{\Delta z}\text{.}\tag{3.1.2} \end{equation}
