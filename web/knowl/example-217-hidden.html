Let two sources of unit strength be located at the points \(\pm 1\text{.}\) The resulting complex potential for a fluid flow is
\begin{equation*} F(z) = \log(z-1) + \log(z+1) =\log(z^2-1)\text{.} \end{equation*}
The velocity potential and stream function are, respectively
\begin{equation*} \phi(x,y) = \ln|z^2-1| \text{ and } \psi(x,y) = \arg(z^2-1)\text{,} \end{equation*}
To solve for the streamline \(\psi(x,y)=c\) we start with
\begin{equation*} c = \arg(z^2-1) = \arg(x^2-y^2-1+i2xy) = \arctan\left(\frac{2xy}{x^2-y^2-1}\right)\text{,} \end{equation*}
and obtain the equation \(x^2+2xy\cot c-y^2=1\text{.}\) If we express this equation in the form
\begin{align*} \left(x-y\tan \frac{c}{2}\right)\left(x+y\cot \frac{c}{2}\right) \amp = 1, \text{ or }\\ \left(x\cos \frac{c}{2}-y\sin \frac{c}{2}\right)\left(x\sin \frac{c}{2} + y\cos \frac{c}{2}\right) \amp = \left(\sin\frac{c}{2}\right)\left(\cos\frac{c}{2}\right)=\frac{\sin c}{2}\text{,} \end{align*}
and use the rotation of axes
\begin{equation*} x^* = x\cos\left(-\frac{c}{2}\right) + y\sin\left(-\frac{c}{2}\right), \text{ and } y^* = -x\sin\left(-\frac{c}{2}\right) + y\cos\left(-\frac{c}{2}\right)\text{,} \end{equation*}
then the streamlines must satisfy the equation \(x^*y^*=\frac{\sin c}{2}\) and are rectangular hyperbolas with centers at the origin that pass through the points \(\pm 1\text{.}\) FigureÂ 10.11.4(b) depicts several streamlines.
