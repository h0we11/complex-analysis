The curl of the vector field in Equation (10.7.1) has magnitude
\begin{equation*} \big|\mathbf{curl}\,\mathbf{V}(x,y)\big| = q_x(x,y) - p_y(x,y) \end{equation*}
and is an indication of how the field swirls in the vicinity of a point. Imagine that a “fluid element” at the point \((x,y)\) is suddenly frozen and then moves freely in the fluid. The fluid element will rotate with an angular velocity given by
\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{2}q_y(x,y) - \frac{1}{2}p_x(x,y) = \frac{1}{2}\big|\mathbf{curl}\,\mathbf{V}(x,y)\big| \end{equation*}
