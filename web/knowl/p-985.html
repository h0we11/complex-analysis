The rule for differentiating polynomials carries over to the complex case as well. If we let \(P(z)\) be a polynomial of degree \(n\text{,}\) so
\begin{equation*} P(z)=a_0 + a_1z + a_2z^2 + \cdots + a_nz^n\text{.} \end{equation*}
then mathematical induction, along with Equations (3.1.5) and (3.1.7), gives
\begin{equation*} P\,'(z) =a_1 + 2a_2z + 3a_3z^2 + \cdots + na_nz^{n-1}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Again, we leave the proof of this result as an exercise.
