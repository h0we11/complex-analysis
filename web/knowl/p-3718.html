Both \(p\) and \(q\) are continuously differentiable, so the mean value theorem implies that
\begin{align} p(x+\Delta x,t) - p(x,t) \amp = p_x(x_1,t) \Delta x, \text{ and }\tag{10.7.3}\\ q(t,y+\Delta y) - q(t,y) \amp = q_y(t,y_2) \Delta y\text{,}\notag \end{align}
where \(x\lt x_1\lt x+\Delta x\) and \(y\lt y_2\lt y+\Delta y\text{.}\) Substitution of the expressions displayed in Equation (10.7.3) into Equation (10.7.2) and subsequently dividing through by \(\Delta x\ \Delta y\) results in
\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{\Delta y}\int_y^{y+\Delta y}p_x(x_1,t)\,dt + \frac{1}{\Delta x}\int_x^{x+\Delta x}q_y(t,y_2)\,dt=0\text{.} \end{equation*}
