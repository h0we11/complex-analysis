If we treat \(v\) as a constant, then this equation represents an ellipse in the \(xy\) plane, the foci occur at the points \((\pm1,0)\text{,}\) and the major axis has length \(2\cosh v\text{.}\) Therefore a point \((x, y)\) on this ellipse must satisfy the equation
\begin{equation*} 2\cosh v = \sqrt{(x+1)^2+y^2}+\sqrt{(x-1)^2+y^2}\text{.} \end{equation*}
The quantity on the right side of this equation is the sum of the distances from \((x,y)\) to \((-1,0)\) and from \((x,y)\) to \((1,0)\text{.}\)
