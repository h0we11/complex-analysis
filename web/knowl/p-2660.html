We use the Maclaurin series for \(\cos z-1\) to write
\begin{equation*} f(z) = \frac{{-}\frac{{1}}{2!}z^2+\frac{1}{4!}z^4-\frac{1}{6!}z^6+\cdots }{z^4}\text{.} \end{equation*}
We formally divide each term by \(z^4\) to obtain the Laurent series
\begin{equation*} f(z) =\frac{-1}{2z^2}+\frac{1}{24}-\frac{z^2}{720}+\cdots, \text{ valid for } z \ne 0\text{.} \end{equation*}
