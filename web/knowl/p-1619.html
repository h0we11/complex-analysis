Method 1: \(\lim\limits_{z \to 0}\frac{ e^{z}-1}{z} = \lim\limits_{z \to 0}\frac{\sum\limits_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n!}z^n}{z} = \lim\limits_{z \to 0}\sum\limits_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n!}z^{n-1} = 1\text{.}\) Justify the last equality. Method 2: Using L’Hôpital’s rule (Theorem 3.1.8), \(\lim\limits_{z \to 0}\frac{e^{z}-1}{z} = \lim\limits_{z \to 0}\frac{e^{z}}{1} = 1\text{.}\)
