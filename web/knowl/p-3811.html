If the case \(\alpha_1 + \alpha_2 + \cdots + \alpha_{n-1} \le \pi\) occurs, then \(\alpha_n>\pi\text{,}\) and the vertices \(w_1,\,w_2,\ldots,w_n\) cannot form a closed polygon. For this case, Equations (10.9.2) and (10.9.3) will determine a mapping from the upper half plane \(\mathrm{Im}(z)>0\) onto an infinite region in the \(w\) plane, where the vertex \(w_n\) is at infinity.
