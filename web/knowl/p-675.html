Because an argument of the product \((z)(z)\) is twice an argument of \(z\text{,}\) we say that \(f\) doubles angles at the origin. Points that lie on the ray \(r>0\text{,}\) \(\theta =\alpha\) are mapped onto points that lie on the ray \(\rho >0\text{,}\) \(\phi =2\alpha\text{.}\) If we now restrict the domain of \(w=f(z) =z^2\) to the region
\begin{equation} A=\{re^{i\theta} : r>0 \text{ and } -\frac{\pi}{2}\lt \theta \le \frac{\pi}{2}\}\text{,}\tag{2.2.1} \end{equation}
then the image of \(A\) under the mapping \(w=z^2\) can be described by the set
\begin{equation} B=\{\rho e^{i\phi} : \rho >0 \text{ and } -\pi \lt \phi \le \pi \}\text{,}\tag{2.2.2} \end{equation}
which consists of all points in the \(w\) plane except the point \(w=0\text{.}\)
