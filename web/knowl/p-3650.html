The condition that a segment of the boundary is insulated can be expressed mathematically by saying that the normal derivative of \(T^*(u,v)\) is zero. That is,
\begin{equation} \frac{\partial T^*}{\partial n}=T_{v}^*(u,0) = 0\text{,}\tag{10.5.10} \end{equation}
where \(n\) is a coordinate measured perpendicular to the segment. We can easily verify that the function
\begin{equation*} T^*(u,v) = T_1+(T_2-T_1)u \end{equation*}
satisfies the conditions stated in Equations (10.5.9) and (10.5.10) for region \(G\text{.}\) Therefore, using Equation (10.5.8), we find that the solution in \(D\) is
\begin{equation*} T(x,y) = T_1 + (T_2-T_1) u(x,y) \end{equation*}
