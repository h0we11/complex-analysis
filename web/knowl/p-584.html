If we let \(K>0\) be a fixed positive real number, then the transformation
\begin{equation*} w=S(z) =Kz=Kx+iKy \end{equation*}
is a one-to-one mapping of the \(z\) plane onto the \(w\) plane and is called a magnification. If \(K>1\text{,}\) it has the effect of stretching the distance between points by the factor \(K\text{.}\) If \(K\lt 1\text{,}\) then it reduces the distance between points by the factor \(K\text{.}\) The inverse transformation is given by
\begin{equation*} z=S^{-1}(w) =\frac{1}{K}w=\frac{1}{K}u+i\frac{1}{K}v \end{equation*}
and shows that \(S\) is one-to-one mapping from the \(z\) plane onto the \(w\) plane. The effect of magnification is shown in FigureÂ 2.1.18.
