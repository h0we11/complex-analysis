Solution 3.2.7.1.

We compute \(u_x(x,y) =v_y(x,y) =3x^2-3y^2\) and \(u_y(x,y) =-6xy=-v_x(x,y)\text{,}\) so the Cauchy-Riemann Equations (3.2.4) are satisfied. Moreover, \(u, \, v, \, u_x, \, u_y, \, v_x\text{,}\) and \(v_y\) are continuous everywhere. By TheoremÂ 3.2.6, \(f\) is differentiable everywhere, and Equation (3.2.2) gives
\begin{equation*} f\,'(z) = u_x(x,y) +iv_x(x,y) = (3x^2-3y^2) +i6xy = 3(x^2-y^2+i2xy) =3z^2\text{.} \end{equation*}
Alternatively, from Equation (3.2.3),
\begin{equation*} f\,'(z) =v_y(x,y) - iu_y(x,y) =(3x^2-3y^2) - i(-6xy) = 3(x^2-y^2+i2xy) = 3z^2\text{.} \end{equation*}
