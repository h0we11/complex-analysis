Definition 2.3.11. Continuity of\(\;\mathbf{f(z)}\).

Let \(f(z)\) be a complex function of the complex variable \(z\) that is defined for all values of \(z\) in some neighborhood of \(z_0\text{.}\) We say that \(f\) is continuous at \(z_0\) if three conditions are satisfied:
\begin{align} \lim\limits_{z \to z_0}f(z) \amp \text{ exists } ,\tag{2.3.10}\\ f(z_0) \amp \text{ exists } , \text{ and }\tag{2.3.11}\\ \lim\limits_{z \to z_0}\,f(z) \amp =f(z_0)\text{.}\tag{2.3.12} \end{align}
