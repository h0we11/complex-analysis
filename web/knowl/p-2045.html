One possible Riemann sum, then, is
\begin{equation*} S(P_8)=\sum_{k=1}^8f(c_k) \Delta z_k = \sum\limits_{k=1}^{8}\exp \left[\frac{2k-1}{8}+i\frac{\pi(2k-1)}{64}\right]\left(\frac{1}{4}+i\frac{\pi}{32}\right)\text{.} \end{equation*}
By rounding the terms in this Riemann sum to two decimal digits, we obtain an approximation for the integral:
\begin{align*} S(P_8) \amp \approx (0.28+0.13i) +(0.33+0.19i) +(0.41+0.29i) +(0.49+0.42i)\\ \amp + (0.57+0.6i) +(0.65+0.84i) +(0.72+1.16i) +(0.78+1.57i)\\ \amp \approx 4.23+5.20i\text{.} \end{align*}
