Let a source and sink of unit strength be located at the points \(+1\) and \(-1\text{,}\) respectively. The complex potential for a fluid flowing from the source at \(+1\) to the sink at \(-1\) is
\begin{equation*} F(z) = \log(z-1) - \log(z+1) = \log\left(\frac{z-1}{z+1}\right)\text{.} \end{equation*}
The velocity potential and stream function are, respectively
\begin{equation*} \phi(x,y) = \ln \left| \frac{z-1}{z+1}\right| \text{ and } \psi(x,y) = \arg\left(\frac{z-1}{z+1}\right)\text{.} \end{equation*}
To solve for the streamline \(\psi(x,y)=c\) we start with
\begin{equation*} c = \arg\left(\frac{z-1}{z+1}\right) = \arg\left(\frac{x^2+y^2-1+i2y}{(x+1)^2+y^2}\right) = \arctan\left(\frac{2y}{x^2+y^2-1}\right)\text{,} \end{equation*}
and obtain the equation \((\tan c)(x^2+y^2-1)=2y\text{.}\) A straightforward calculation shows that points on the streamline must satisfy the equation
\begin{equation*} x^2+(y-\cot c)^2=1+\cot ^2c\text{,} \end{equation*}
which is the equation of a circle with center at \((0, \cot c)\) that passes through the points \((\pm 1, 0)\text{.}\) Several streamlines are indicated in FigureÂ 10.11.4(a).
