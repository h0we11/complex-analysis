Using \(z=re^{i\theta}\) in the expression of a complex function \(f\) may be convenient. It gives us the polar representation
\begin{equation} f(z) = f(re^{i\theta}) =u(r,\theta) +iv(r,\theta)\text{,}\tag{2.1.2} \end{equation}
where \(u\) and \(v\) are real functions of the real variables \(r\) and \(\theta\text{.}\)
