Next, we use the change of variable \(h=r\tan t\) and \(dh=r\sec^2t\,dt\) and the trigonometric identity \(\sec ^2t=\frac{r^2+h^2}{r^2}\) to obtain the equivalent integral:
\begin{equation*} |\mathbf{E}(x,y)| =\int_{-\frac{\pi}{2}}^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{(\frac{q}{2}) \cos t}{r^2+h^2}\frac{r^2+h^2}{r}\,dt = \frac{q}{2r}\int_{-\frac{\pi}{2}}^\frac{\pi}{2}\cos t\,dt=\frac{q}{r}\text{.} \end{equation*}
