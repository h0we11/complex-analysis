Since \(\phi_x=p\) and \(\phi_y=q\text{,}\) we also have
\begin{equation*} \nabla \phi(x,y) = p(x,y) + iq(x,y) = \mathbf{V}(x,y)\text{,} \end{equation*}
so \(\phi(x,y)\) is the velocity potential for the flow, and the curves
\begin{equation*} \phi(x,y) = K_1 \end{equation*}
are called equipotentials. The function \(\psi(x,y)\) is called the stream function. The curves
\begin{equation*} \psi(x,y) = K_2 \end{equation*}
are called streamlines and describe the paths of the fluid particles. To demonstrate this result, we implicitly differentiate \(\psi(x,y)=K_2\) and find that the slope of a vector tangent is given by
\begin{equation*} \frac{dy}{dx} = -\frac{\psi_x(x,y)}{\psi_y(x,y)}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Using the fact that \(\psi_y=\phi_x\) and this equation, we find that the tangent vector to the curve is
\begin{equation*} \mathbf{T} = \phi_x(x,y) - i\psi_x(x,y) = p(x,y) + iq(x,y) = \mathbf{V}(x,y) \end{equation*}
