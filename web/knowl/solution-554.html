Solution 8.7.14.1.

Let \(f(z) =7z+1\text{,}\) then \(f(z)+g(z)=z^4\text{.}\) At points on the circle \(C_1(0) =\{z:|z| =1\}\) we have the relation
\begin{equation*} |f(z)+g(z)| = |z^4| = 1 \lt 6 = |7| - |1| \le |7z+1| = |f(z)|\text{.} \end{equation*}
The function \(f\) has one zero at the point \(-\frac{1}{7}\) in the disk \(D_1(0)\text{,}\) and the hypotheses of CorollaryÂ 8.7.11 hold on the circle \(C_1(0)\text{.}\) Therefore \(g\) has one zero inside \(D_1(0)\text{.}\)
