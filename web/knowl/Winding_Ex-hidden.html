The image of the circle \(C_2(0)\) under \(f(z)=z^2+z\) is the curve given by \(\{(x,y) =(4\cos 2t+2\cos t,4\sin 2t+2\sin t) :0\lt t\lt 2\pi \}\) shown in FigureÂ 8.7.7.
Note that the image curve \(f\big(C_2(0)\big)\) winds twice around the origin. We check this by computing \(\frac{1}{2\pi i}\int_{C_2^{+}(0)}\frac{f\,'(z)}{f(z)}\,dz = \frac{1}{2\pi i}\int_{C_2^{+}(0)}\frac{ 2z+1}{z^2+z}dz\text{.}\) The residues of the integrand are at \(0\) and \(-1\text{.}\) Thus
\begin{align*} \frac{1}{2\pi i}\int_{C_2^{+}(0)}\frac{2z+1}{z^2+z}dz \amp = \mathrm{Res}\left[\frac{2z+1}{z^2+z},0\right] +\mathrm{Res}\left[\frac{2z+1}{z^2+z},-1\right]\\ \amp = 1+1\\ \amp = 2\text{.} \end{align*}
