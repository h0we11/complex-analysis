We can easily find the inverse mapping and express it similarly:
\begin{equation*} z = g_1(w) = (w^2+1)^\frac{1}{2} = (w+i)^\frac{1}{2}(w-i)^\frac{1}{2}\text{,} \end{equation*}
where the branches of the square root function are given by
\begin{equation*} (w+i)^\frac{1}{2}=\sqrt{\rho_1}e^{i\frac{\phi_1}{2}}\text{,} \end{equation*}
where \(\rho_1=|w+i|, \; \phi_1 = \arg_{-\frac{\pi}{2}}(w+i)\text{,}\) and \({-\frac{\pi}{2}} \lt \arg_{-\frac{\pi}{2}}(w+i) \lt \frac{3\pi}{2}\text{,}\) and
\begin{equation*} (w-i)^\frac{1}{2} = \sqrt{\rho_2}e^{i\frac{\phi_2}{2}}\text{,} \end{equation*}
where \(\rho_2 = |w-i|, \; \phi_2 = \arg_{-\frac{\pi}{2}}(w-i)\text{,}\) and \(-\frac{\pi}{2} \lt \arg_{-\frac{\pi}{2}}(w-i) \lt \frac{3\pi}{2}\text{.}\)
