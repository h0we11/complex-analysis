Let \(u(x, y)\) be a real-valued function of the two real variables \(x\) and \(y\text{.}\) We say that \(u\) is continuous at the point \((x_0,\,y_0)\) if three conditions are satisfied:
\begin{align} \lim_{(x,y) \to (x_0,y_0)}u(x, y) \amp \text{ exists, }\tag{2.3.7}\\ u(x_0,\,y_0) \amp \text{ exists } , \qquad \text{ and }\tag{2.3.8}\\ \lim_{(x,y) \to (x_0,y_0)}u(x, y) \amp = u(x_0,\,y_0) \text{.}\tag{2.3.9} \end{align}
