Let \(f(t) = \sin t\) and \(F(s) =\frac{1}{s^2+1}\text{.}\) Since \(\lim\limits_{t \to 0^+}\frac{\sin t}{t}=1\text{,}\) we can integrate \(F(s)\) to obtain the desired result:
\begin{equation*} \mathcal{L}\left(\frac{\sin t}{t}\right) = \int_s^{\infty}\frac{d\sigma}{\sigma^2+1} = \left.-\arctan \frac{1}{\sigma }\right|_{\sigma =s}^{\sigma =\infty} = \arctan \frac{1}{s}\text{.} \end{equation*}
