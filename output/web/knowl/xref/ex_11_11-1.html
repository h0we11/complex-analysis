Exercise 1.
Let the coordinate axes be walls of a containing vessel for a fluid flow in the first quadrant that is produced by a source of unit strength located at \(z_1=1\) and a sink of unit strength located at \(z_2=i\text{.}\) Show that \(F(z) =\log\left(\frac{z^2-1}{z^2+1}\right)\) is the complex potential for the flow shown in FigureÂ 10.11.12.
Solution.
\(F_{1}(w)=\log(\frac{w-1}{w+1})\) is the complex potential for a source at \(w_1=1\) and sink at \(w_2=-1\text{.}\) The function \(w=S(z)=z^2\) maps \(z_1=1\) and \(z_2=i\) onto \(w_1\) and \(w_2\text{,}\) respectively. Therefore, the composition \(F_2(z) = F_1\big(S(z)\big) = F_1(z^2) = \log(\frac{z^2-1}{z^2+1})\) is the desired complex potential.
