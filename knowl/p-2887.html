The Cauchy integral formulas given in SectionÂ 6.5 are useful in evaluating contour integrals over a simple closed contour \(C\) where the integrand has the form \(\frac{f(z)}{(z-z_0)^k}\) and \(f\) is an analytic function. In this case, the singularity of the integrand is at worst a pole of order \(k\) at \(z_0\text{.}\) We begin this section by extending this result to integrals that have a finite number of isolated singularities inside the contour \(C\text{.}\) This new method can be used in cases where the integrand has an essential singularity at \(z_0\) and is an important extension of the previous method.
