Solution 2.3.8.1.

Let
\begin{equation*} f(z) =z^2-2z+1=x^2-y^2-2x+1+i(2xy-2y)\text{.} \end{equation*}
Computing the limits for \(u\) and \(v\text{,}\) we obtain
\begin{align*} \lim\limits_{(x,y) \to (1,1)}u(x, y) \amp =1-1-2+1=-1\ \ \text{ and }\\ \lim\limits_{(x,y) \to (1,1)}v(x, y) \amp =2-2=0\text{,} \end{align*}
so our previous theorem implies that \(\lim\limits_{z \to 1+i}\) \(f(z) = -1\text{.}\)
