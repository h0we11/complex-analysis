Similarly, we can start with Equation (9.4.3) and obtain the equations
\begin{equation*} \sin u = \frac{x}{\cosh v}, \text{ and } \cos u = \frac{y}{\sinh v}\text{.} \end{equation*}
We then eliminate \(u\) from these equations and obtain the single equation
\begin{equation*} \frac{x^2}{\cosh^2v} + \frac{y^2}{\sinh^2v} = 1\text{.} \end{equation*}
