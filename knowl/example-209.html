Example 10.7.6.

Consider the complex potential \(F(z) = \frac{A}{2}z^2\text{,}\) where \(A\) is a positive real number. The velocity potential and stream function are given by
\begin{equation*} \phi(x,y) = \frac{A}{2}(x^2-y^2) \text{ and } \psi(x,y) = Axy\text{,} \end{equation*}
respectively. The streamlines
\begin{equation*} \psi(x,y)= \text{ constant } \end{equation*}
form a family of hyperbolas with asymptotes along the coordinate axes.
