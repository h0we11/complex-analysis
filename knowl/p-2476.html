To prove part (ii), let \(\varepsilon>0\) be given and let \(L\) be the length of the contour \(C\text{.}\) Because \(\{S_k\}\) converges uniformly to \(f\) on \(T\text{,}\) there exists a positive integer \(N_{\varepsilon}\) such that, if \(k \ge N_{\varepsilon}\text{,}\) then \(|S_k(z) -f(z)| \lt \frac{\varepsilon}{L}\) for all \(z \in T\text{.}\) Because \(C\) is contained in \(T\text{,}\) \(\max\limits_{z \in C} \big|S_k(z) -f(z)\big| \lt \frac{\varepsilon}{L}\) if \(k \ge N_{\varepsilon}\text{,}\) and we can use the ML inequality (TheoremÂ 6.2.19) to get
\begin{align*} \left|\int_CS_k(z)\,dz- \int_{C}f(z)\,dz\right| \amp = \left|\int_C[S_k(z)-f(z)]\,dz\right|\\ \amp \le \max_{z \in C}\big|S_k(z) -f(z)\big| L\\ \amp \lt \left(\frac{\varepsilon}{L}\right) L\\ \amp = \varepsilon\text{.} \end{align*}
