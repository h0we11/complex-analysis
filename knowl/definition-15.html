Definition 2.3.1. Limit of \(\mathbf{u(x,y)}\).

The expression \(\lim\limits_{(x,y) \to (x_0,y_0)}u(x, y)=u_0\) means that for each number \(\varepsilon > 0\text{,}\) there is a corresponding number \(\delta > 0\) such that
\begin{equation} |u(x, y) - u_0| \lt \varepsilon \text{ whenever } 0\lt \sqrt{(x-x_0)^2+(y-y_0)^2} \lt \delta\text{.}\tag{2.3.1} \end{equation}
