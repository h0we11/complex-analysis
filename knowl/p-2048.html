It follows that
\begin{align*} \lim\limits_{n \to \infty}\sum_{k=1}^nf(c_k) \Delta z_k \amp = \lim_{n \to \infty}\sum\limits_{k=1}^nf(c_k) (z_k-z_{k-1})\\ \amp = \lim\limits_{n \to \infty}\sum\limits_{k=1}^nf(z(\tau_k)) \big[z(t_k)-z(t_{k-1})\big]\text{.} \end{align*}
where \(\tau _k\) and \(t_k\) are the points contained in the interval \([a,bt]\) with the property that \(c_k=z(\tau _k)\) and \(z_k=z(t_k)\text{,}\) as is also shown in FigureÂ 6.2.10. If for all \(k\) we multiply the \(k\)th term in the last sum by \(\frac{{t_k-t_{k-1}} }{{t_k-t_{k-1}}}\text{,}\) then we get
\begin{align*} \amp \lim_{n \to \infty}\sum\limits_{k=1}^nf(z(\tau_k))\left[ \frac{z(t_k) -z(t_{k-1})}{t_k-t_{k-1}}\right] (t_k-t_{k-1}) =\\ \amp \lim\limits_{n \to \infty}\sum\limits_{k=1}^nf(z(\tau_k)) \left[\frac{z(t_k) -z(t_{k-1})}{t_k-t_{k-1}}\right] \Delta t_k\text{.} \end{align*}
