The formulation of limits in terms of open disks provides a good context for looking at this definition. It says that for each disk of radius \(\varepsilon\) about the point \(w_0\) (represented by \(D_{\varepsilon}(w_0)\)) there is a punctured disk of radius \(\delta\) about the point \(z_0\) (represented by \(D_{\delta}^*(z_0)\)) such that the image of each point in the punctured \(\delta\) disk lies in the \(\varepsilon\) disk. The image of the \(\delta\) disk does not have to fill up the entire \(\varepsilon\) disk; but if \(z\) approaches \(z_0\) along a curve that ends at \(z_0\text{,}\) then \(w=f(z)\) approaches \(w_0\text{.}\) The situation is illustrated in FigureÂ 2.3.5.
