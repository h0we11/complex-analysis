We consider only fluid flows for which the curl is zero. Such fluid flows are called irrotational. This condition is more precisely characterized by requiring that the line integral of the tangential component of \(\mathbf{V}(x,y)\) along any simply closed contour be identically zero. If we consider the rectangle in FigureÂ 10.7.1, then the tangential component is given by \(p\) on the bottom edge, \(q\) on the right edge, \(-p\) on the top edge, and \(-q\) on the left edge. Integrating and equating the resulting circulation integral to zero yields
\begin{equation*} \int_y^{y+\Delta y}[q(x+\Delta x,t) - q(x,t)]\,dt - \int_x^{x+\Delta x}[p(t, y+\Delta y) - p(t,y)]\,dt=0\text{.} \end{equation*}
