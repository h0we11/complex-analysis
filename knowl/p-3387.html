The half rays \(\{\mathrm{Arg}(w)=c\}\text{,}\) where \(c\) is a constant, that meet at the origin \(w=0\) represent the lines of electric flux produced by a source located at \(w=0\) (and a sink at \(w=\infty\)). The preimage of this family of lines is a family of circles that pass through the points \(z=0\) and \(z=a\text{.}\) We visualize these circles as the lines of electric flux from one point charge to another.
