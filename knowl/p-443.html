Use part (a) and De Moivre’s formula to derive Lagrange’s identity, which shows that, for \(0 \lt \theta \lt 2\pi\text{,}\)
\begin{equation*} 1+\cos \theta +\cos 2\theta +\cdots +\cos n\theta = \frac{1}{2}+\frac{\sin\left[(n+\frac{1}{2})\theta \right]}{2\sin\frac{\theta}{2}} \end{equation*}
