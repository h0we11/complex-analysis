Let \(m\) and \(n\) be positive integers that have no common factor. Show that there are \(n\) distinct solutions to \(w^n=z^m\text{,}\) given by
\begin{equation} w_k=r^\frac{m}{n}\left(\cos \frac{m(\theta +2\pi k)}{n}+i\sin \frac{m(\theta +2\pi k)}{n}\right) \; \text{ for } \; k=0, 1, \ldots, n-1\text{.}\tag{1.5.9} \end{equation}
