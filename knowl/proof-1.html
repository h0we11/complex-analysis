Proof

We appeal to basic results:
\begin{align*} |z_1+z_2|^2 \amp = (z_1+z_2) \overline{(z_1+z_2)} \amp \amp \text{ (by Identity (1.3.3)) }\\ \amp = (z_1+z_2) (\overline{z_1}+\overline{z_2}) \amp \amp \text{ (by Identity (1.2.11)) }\\ \amp = z_1\overline{z_1} + z_1\overline{z_2} + z_2\overline{z_1} +z_2\overline{z_2} \amp \amp\\ \amp = |z_1|^2 + z_1\overline{z_2}+\overline{z_1}z_2 + |z_2|^2 \amp \amp \text{ (by Identity (1.3.3) and the commutative law) }\\ \amp = |z_1|^2 + z_1 \overline{z_2}+\overline{(z_1\overline{z_2})} + |z_2|^2 \amp \amp \text{ (by Identities (1.2.10) and (1.2.12)) }\\ \amp = |z_1|^2 + 2 \mathrm{Re}(z_1 \overline{z_2}) + |z_2|^2 \amp \amp \text{ (by Identity (1.2.14)) }\\ \amp \le |z_1|^2 + 2|z_1\overline{z_2}| + |z_2|^2 \amp \amp \text{ (by Identity (1.3.2)) }\\ \amp = (|z_1| + |z_2|)^2\text{.} \end{align*}
Taking square roots yields the desired inequality.
