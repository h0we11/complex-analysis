If we use Green’s theorem on the real part of the right side of Equation (6.3.4) (with \(P=u\) and \(Q=-v)\text{,}\) we obtain
\begin{equation} \int_Cu\,dx - v\,dy = \iint\limits_R(-v_x-u_y)\,dx\,dy\text{,}\tag{6.3.5} \end{equation}
where \(R\) is the region that is the interior of \(C\text{.}\) If we use Green’s theorem on the imaginary part, we get
\begin{equation} \int_Cv\,dx +u\,dy = \iint\limits_R(u_x-v_y)\,dx\,dy\text{.}\tag{6.3.6} \end{equation}
