Pay careful attention to the value \(\Delta z\) in Equation (3.1.3); the limit must be independent of the manner in which \(\Delta z \to 0\text{.}\) If we can find two curves that end at \(z_0\) along which \(\frac{\Delta w}{\Delta z}\) approaches distinct values, then \(\frac{ \Delta w}{\Delta z}\) does not have a limit as \(\Delta z \to 0\text{,}\) so \(f\) does not have a derivative at \(z_0\text{.}\) The same observation applies to the limits in Equations (3.1.1) and (3.1.2).
