We choose two approaches to the point \(z_0=x_0+iy_0\) and compute limits of the difference quotients. First, we approach \(z_0=x_0+iy_0\) along a line parallel to the \(x\)-axis by forcing \(z\) to be of the form \(z=x+iy_0\text{.}\)
\begin{align*} \lim\limits_{z \to z_0}\frac{f(z) -f(z_0)}{z-z_0} \amp = \lim\limits_{(x+iy_0) \to (x_0+iy_0)} \frac{f(x+iy_0) - f(x_0+iy_0)}{(x+iy_0)-(x_0+iy_0)}\\ \amp = \lim\limits_{(x+iy_0) \to (x_0+iy_0)}\frac{(x-iy_0) -(x_0-iy_0)}{(x-x_0) +i(y_0-y_0)}\\ \amp = \lim\limits_{(x+iy_0) \to (x_0+iy_0)}\frac{x-x_0}{x-x_0}\\ \amp = 1\text{.} \end{align*}
