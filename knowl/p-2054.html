We must compute \(\int_C\exp z\) \(dz\text{,}\) where \(C\) is the line segment joining \(A=0\) to \(B=2+i\frac{\pi}{4}\text{.}\) According to Equation (1.6.2), we can parametrize \(C\) by \(z(t)=(2+i\frac{ \pi}{4}) t\text{,}\) for \(0 \le t \le 1\text{.}\) As \(z\,'(t) = (2+i\frac{\pi}{4})\text{,}\) Theorem 6.2.11 guarantees that
\begin{align*} \int_C\exp z\,dz \amp = \int_0^1\exp[z(t)] z\,'(t)\,dt\\ \amp = \int_0^1\exp \left[\left(2+i\frac{\pi}{4}\right)t\right]\left(2+i\frac{\pi}{4}\right)dt\\ \amp = \left(2+i\frac{\pi}{4}\right) \int_0^1e^{2t}e^{i\frac{\pi}{4}t}\,dt\\ \amp = \left(2+i\frac{\pi}{4}\right) \int_0^1e^{2t}\left(\cos\frac{\pi t}{4}+i\sin \frac{\pi t}{4}\right)dt\\ \amp = \left(2+i\frac{\pi}{4}\right) \left(\int_0^1e^{2t}\cos \frac{\pi t}{4}\,dt+i\int_0^1e^{2t}\sin \frac{\pi t}{4}\,dt\right)\text{.} \end{align*}
