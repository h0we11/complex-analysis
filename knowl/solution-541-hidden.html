We use the function \(f(z) =\frac{\mathrm{\log }_{-\frac{\pi}{2}}z}{z^2+a^2}\text{.}\) Recall that
\begin{equation*} \log_{-\frac{\pi}{2}}z = \ln |z| + i\arg_{-\frac{\pi}{2}}z = \ln r+i\theta\text{,} \end{equation*}
where \(z=re^{i\theta}\ne 0\) and \(-\frac{\pi}{2}\lt \theta \le \frac{3\pi}{2}\text{.}\) The path \(C\) of integration will consist of the segments \([-R,-r ]\) and \([r,R]\) of the \(x\)-axis together with the upper semicircles \(C_{r}:z=re^{i\theta}\) and \(C_R:z=Re^{i\theta}\text{,}\) for \(0 \le \theta \le \pi\text{,}\) as shown in FigureÂ 8.6.5.
