\begin{equation*} f(z) = \begin{cases}\frac{1}{1-z}, \amp \text{ when } z \ne \frac{1}{2}; \\ \;\; 0, \amp \text{ when } z = \frac{1}{2}. \end{cases} \end{equation*}
(a)
Use Theorem 7.2.3, Taylor’s theorem, to show that the Maclaurin series for \(f(z)\) equals \(\sum\limits_{n=0}^{\infty}z^n\text{.}\)
