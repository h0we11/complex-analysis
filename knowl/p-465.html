We need to develop some vocabulary that will help describe sets of points in the plane. One fundamental idea is that of an \(\varepsilon\)-neighborhood of the point \(z_0\text{.}\) It is the open disk of radius \(\varepsilon>0\) about \(z_0\) shown in FigureÂ 1.6.6. Formally, it is the set of all points satisfying the inequality \(\{z: |z-z_0| \lt \varepsilon\}\) and is denoted by \(D_{\varepsilon}(z_0)\text{.}\) That is,
\begin{equation} D_{\varepsilon}(z_0) = \{z: |z-z_0| \lt \varepsilon \}\text{.}\tag{1.6.4} \end{equation}
