We can represent the number \(z=x+iy=(x, y)\) by a position vector in the \(xy\) plane whose tail is at the origin and whose head is at the point \((x, y)\text{.}\) When the \(xy\) plane is used for displaying complex numbers, it is called the complex plane, or more simply, the z plane. Recall that \(\mathrm{Re}(z)=x\) and \(\mathrm{Im}(z)=y\text{.}\) Geometrically, \(\mathrm{Re}(z)\) is the projection of \(z=(x, y)\) onto the \(x\) axis, and \(\mathrm{Im}(z)\) is the projection of \(z\) onto the \(y\) axis.
