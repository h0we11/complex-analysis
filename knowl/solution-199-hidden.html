We can write the fluid flow expression as
\begin{equation*} \mathbf{V}(x,y) = \overline{f(z)}= \overline{2x+i2y}=\overline{2z}\text{.} \end{equation*}
An antiderivative of \(f(z) =2z\) is \(F(z)=z^2\text{,}\) and the real part of \(F(z)\) is the desired harmonic function:
\begin{equation*} \phi (x,y) = \mathrm{Re}\left[ F(z) \right] = \mathrm{Im}\left[ x^2-y^2+i2xy\right] =x^2-y^2\text{.} \end{equation*}
