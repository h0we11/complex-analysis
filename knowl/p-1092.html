Recall (DefinitionÂ 3.1.4) that when we say a function is analytic at a point \(z_0\) we mean that the function is differentiable not only at \(z_0\text{,}\) but also at every point in some \(\varepsilon\)-neighborhood of \(z_0\text{.}\) With this in mind, we proceed to determine where the Cauchy-Riemann equations are satisfied. We write \(u(x,y) =x^3+3xy^2\) and \(v(x,y) =y^3+3x^2y\) and compute the partial derivatives:
\begin{align*} u_x(x,y) \amp = 3x^2+3y^2, \; v_y(x,y) = 3x^2+3y^2, \text{ and }\\ u_y(x,y) \amp = 6xy, \; v_x(x,y) = 6xy\text{.} \end{align*}
