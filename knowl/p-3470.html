The curve given by Equation (9.4.1) is identified as a hyperbola in the \(uv\) plane that has foci at the points \((\pm1,0)\text{.}\) Therefore the vertical line \(x=a\) is mapped one-to-one onto the branch of the hyperbola given by Equation (9.4.1) that passes through the point \((\sin a,0)\text{.}\) If   \(0\lt a\lt \frac{\pi}{2}\text{,}\) then it is the right branch; if \(-\frac{\pi}{2}\lt a\lt 0\text{,}\) it is the left branch. The image of the \(y\)-axis, which is the line \(x=0\text{,}\) is the \(v\)-axis. The images of several vertical lines are shown in Figure 9.4.4(a).
