The integrand \(f(z) =\frac{1}{(z^2+4)^3}\) has a pole of order \(3\) at the point \(2i\text{,}\) which is the only singularity of \(f\) in the upper half-plane. Computing the residue, we get
\begin{align*} \mathrm{Res}[f,2i] \amp = \frac{1}{2}\lim\limits_{z \to 2i}\frac{d^2}{dz^2}\left[\frac{1}{(z+2i)^3}\right]\\ \amp = \frac{1}{2}\lim_{z \to 2i}\frac{d}{dz}\left[\frac{-3}{(z+2i)^4}\right]\\ \amp = \frac{1}{2}\lim\limits_{z \to 2i}\left[\frac{12}{(z+2i)^5}\right]\\ \amp = -\frac{3i}{512}\text{.} \end{align*}
