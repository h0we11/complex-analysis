The curve given by Equation (9.4.2) is identified as an ellipse in the \(uv\) plane that passes through the points \((\pm\cosh b,0)\) and \((0,\pm\sinh b)\) and has foci at the points \((\pm1,0)\text{.}\) Therefore, if \(b>0\text{,}\) then \(v = \cos x\sinh b > 0\text{,}\) and the image of the horizontal segment is the portion of the ellipse given by Equation (9.4.2) that lies in the upper half-plane \(\mathrm{Im}(w) > 0\text{.}\) If \(b\lt 0\text{,}\) then it is the portion that lies in the lower half-plane.
