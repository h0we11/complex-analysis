Thus, the mapping \(w=\mathrm{Arcsin}\,z\) is a one-to-one conformal mapping of the \(z\) plane cut along the rays \(x \le-1, \; y=0\text{,}\) and \(x \ge 1, \; y=0\text{,}\) onto the vertical strip \(-\frac{\pi}{2} \le u \le \frac{\pi}{2}\) in the \(w\)-plane, which can be construed from Figure 9.4.4 if we interchange the roles of the \(z\) and \(w\) planes. The image of the square \(0 \le x \le 4, \; 0 \le y \le 4\text{,}\) under \(w=\mathrm{Arcsin}\,z\text{,}\) is shown in Figure 9.4.5. We obtained it by plotting the two families of curves \(\{(u(c,t), \; v(c,t)) : 0 \le t \le 4\}\) and \(\{(u(t,c), \; v(t,c)) : 0 \le t \le 4\}\text{,}\) where \(c=\frac{k}{5}\text{,}\) \(k=0,1,\ldots,20\text{.}\) The formulas in Equations (9.4.4) and (9.4.5) are also convenient for evaluating \(\mathrm{Arcsin}(z)\text{,}\) as shown in Example 9.4.6
