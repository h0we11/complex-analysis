Suppose that two parallel planes are perpendicular to the \(z\) plane and pass through the horizontal lines \(y=a\) and \(y=b\) and that the temperature is held constant at the values \(T(x,a)=T_1\) and \(T(x,b) =T_2\text{,}\) respectively, on these planes. Then \(T\) is given by
\begin{equation*} T(x,y) =T_1+\frac{T_2-T_1}{b-a}(y-a) \end{equation*}
Solution.
The isothermals \(T(x,y) =\alpha\) are easily seen to be horizontal lines. The conjugate harmonic function is
\begin{equation*} S(x,y) =\frac{T_1-T_2}{b-a}x\text{,} \end{equation*}
and the heat flow lines \(S(x,y) =\beta\) are vertical segments between the horizontal lines. If \(T_1>T_2\text{,}\) then the heat flows along these segments from the plane through \(y=a\) to the plane through \(y=b\text{,}\) as illustrated in FigureÂ 10.5.3.
