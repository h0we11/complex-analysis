Since \(\lim\limits_{z \to z_0}\eta (z)=0\text{,}\) we know that given \(\varepsilon >0\text{,}\) we can find \(\delta >0\) such that
\begin{equation} |z-z_0| \lt \delta \text{ implies that } |\eta (z)| \lt \frac{2}{L^2} \varepsilon\text{,}\tag{6.3.10} \end{equation}
where \(L\) is the length of the original contour \(C\text{.}\) We can now choose an integer \(n\) so that \(C_n\) lies in the neighborhood \(z-z_0| \lt \delta\text{,}\) as shown in Figure 6.21.
