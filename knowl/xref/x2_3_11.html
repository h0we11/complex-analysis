Exercise 11.

Let \(f(z)=z^{\frac{1}{2}}=r^{\frac{1}{2}}(\cos \frac{\theta}{2}+i\sin \frac{\theta}{2})\text{,}\) where \(z=re^{i\theta}\text{,}\) \(r>0\text{,}\) and \(-\pi \lt \theta \le \pi\text{.}\) Use the polar form of \(z\) and show that
(a)
\(f(z) \to i\) as \(z \to -1\) along the upper semicircle \(r=1\text{,}\) \(0\lt \theta \le \pi\text{.}\)
Solution.
If \(z \to -1\) along the upper semicircle \(r=1, \; 0\lt \theta \le \pi\text{,}\) then \(\lim\limits_{z \to -1}f(z) = \lim\limits_{\theta \to \pi}[\cos(\frac{\theta}{2}) +i\sin (\frac{\theta}{2})] = \cos(\frac{\pi}{2}) +i\sin(\frac{\pi}{2})=i\text{.}\)
(b)
\(f(z) \to -i\) as \(z \to -1\) along the lower semicircle \(r=1\text{,}\) \(-\pi \lt \theta \lt 0\text{.}\)
