Corollary 7.5.4.

(L’Hôpital’s rule) Suppose that \(f\) and \(g\) are analytic at \(\alpha\text{.}\) If \(f(\alpha)=0\) and \(g(\alpha)=0\text{,}\) but \(g\,'(\alpha) \ne 0\text{,}\) then
\begin{equation*} \lim_{z \to \alpha }\frac{f(z)}{g(z)} = \frac{f\,'(\alpha)}{g'(\alpha)} \end{equation*}
