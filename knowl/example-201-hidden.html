Find the temperature \(T(x,y)\) at each point in the upper half-plane \(\mathrm{Im}(z) > 0\) if the temperature along the \(x\) axis satisfies
\begin{equation*} T(x,0) = T_1 \text{ for } x>0, \text{ and } T(x,0) = T_2 \text{ for } x\lt 0\text{.} \end{equation*}
Solution.
The isotherms \(T(x,y) =\alpha\) are rays emanating from the origin. The conjugate harmonic function is \(S(x,y) = \frac{1}{\pi}(T_1-T_2)\) \(\ln \,|z|\text{,}\) and the heat flow lines \(S(x,y) =\beta\) are semicircles centered at the origin.
