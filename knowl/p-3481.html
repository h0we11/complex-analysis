The function \(z=\sin w\) maps points in the upper half (lower half) of the vertical strip \(\frac{-\pi}{2} \lt u \lt \frac{\pi}{2}\) onto the upper half-plane (lower half-plane), respectively. Hence we can solve the preceding equation and obtain \(v\) as a function of \(x\) and \(y\text{:}\)
\begin{equation} v(x,y) = (\mathrm{sign}\,y) \mathrm{Arccosh}\left[\frac{\sqrt{(x+1)^2+y^2} + \sqrt{(x-1)^2+y^2}}{2}\right]\text{,}\tag{9.4.5} \end{equation}
where \(\mathrm{sign}\,y =1\text{,}\) if \(y \ge 0\text{,}\) and \(\mathrm{sign}\,y= -1\text{,}\) if \(y\lt 0\text{.}\) In Equation (9.4.5) we use the real function given by \(\mathrm{Arccosh}(t) = \ln(t+\sqrt{t^2-1})\text{,}\) with \(t \ge 1\text{.}\)
