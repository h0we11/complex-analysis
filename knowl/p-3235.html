Let \(a_1, \, a_2, \, \ldots, \, a_{Z_f}\) be the zeros of \(f\) inside \(C\) counted according to multiplicity and let \(b_1, \, b_2, \, \ldots, \, b_{P_f}\) be the poles of \(f\) inside \(C\) counted according to multiplicity. Then \(f(z)\) has the representation
\begin{equation*} f(z) = \frac{(z-a_1) (z-a_2) \cdots (z-a_{Z_f})}{(z-b_1)(z-b_2) \cdots (z-b_{P_f})}g(z)\text{,} \end{equation*}
where \(g\) is analytic and nonzero on \(C\) and inside \(C\text{.}\) A standard calculation shows that
\begin{align} \frac{f\,'(z)}{f(z)} \amp = \frac{1}{(z-a_1)} + \frac{1}{(z-a_2)} + \cdots + \frac{1}{(z-a_{Z_f})}\notag\\ \amp - \frac{1}{(z-b_1)} - \frac{1}{(z-b_2)} - \cdots - \frac{1}{(z-b_{P_f})} + \frac{g\,'(z)}{g(z)}\text{.}\tag{8.7.2} \end{align}
