Equation (10.7.4) and this equation show that the function \(f(z) = p(x,y) - iq(x,y)\) satisfies the Cauchy-Riemann equations and is an analytic function. If we let \(F(z)\) denote the antiderivative of \(f(z)\text{,}\) then
\begin{equation} F(z) = \phi(x,y) +i\psi(x,y)\text{,}\tag{10.7.5} \end{equation}
which is the complex potential of the flow and has the property
\begin{equation*} \overline{F,'(z)} = \phi_x(x,y) - i\psi_x(x,y) = p(x,y) + iq(x,y) = \mathbf{V}(x,y) \end{equation*}
