If \(w=S(z)\) is a conformal mapping and \(w_0=S(z_0)\text{,}\) then \(S(z)\) has a nonzero derivative at \(z_0\text{,}\) and
\begin{equation*} w-w_0=(z-z_0)[ S\,'(z_0) +\eta(z)]\text{,} \end{equation*}
where \(\eta(z) \to 0\) as \(z \to z_0\text{.}\) Taking logarithms yields
\begin{equation*} \log(w-w_0) = \log(z-z_0) + \mathrm{Log}[S\,'(z_0) + \eta (z)]\text{.} \end{equation*}
Because \(S\,'(z_0) \ne 0\text{,}\) the term \(\mathrm{Log}[S\,'(z_0) + \eta(z)]\) approaches the constant value \(\mathrm{Log}[S\,'(z_0)]\) as \(z \to z_0\text{.}\) Also, because \(\log (z-z_0)\) is the complex potential for a source located at the point \(z_0\text{,}\) the image of a source under a conformal mapping is a source.
