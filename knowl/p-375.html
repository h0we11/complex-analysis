An interesting application of the laws of exponents comes from putting the equation \((e^{i\theta})^n=e^{in\theta}\) in its polar form. Doing so gives
\begin{equation} (\cos \theta +i\sin \theta)^n = (\cos n\theta +i\sin n\theta)\text{,}\tag{1.5.2} \end{equation}
which is known as De Moivre’s formula, in honor of the French mathematician Abraham De Moivre (1667–1754).
