Suppose that \(f\) is analytic at each point on a simple closed contour \(C\) and \(f\) is meromorphic in the domain that is the interior of \(C\text{.}\) We assert without proof that TheoremÂ 7.5.1 can be extended to meromorphic functions so that \(f\) has at most a finite number of zeros that lie inside \(C\text{.}\) Since the function \(g(z)=\frac{1}{f(z)}\) is also meromorphic, it can have only a finite number of zeros inside \(C\text{,}\) and so \(f\) can have at most a finite number of poles that lie inside \(C\text{.}\)
